Amicable Numbers

A pair of numbers are called amicable if their factors (excluding themselves) add up to each other.
For example, the numbers 220 and 284 are amicable, because the factors of 220 are [1, 2, 4, 5, 10, 11, 20, 22, 44, 55, 110]
and sum to 284, while the factors of 284 are [1, 2, 4, 71, 142] and sum to 220.
